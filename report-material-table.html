Learning Outcomes

Early Learning Goals:

Learns to mix with similar age group, develop the confidence to speak, maintain focus and attention span Starts to make friends and be able to grasp in an interactive manner. Learns to involve in topics being taught and share thoughts with classmates and teacher.

Material Required ( additional info)
Musical Game: Make the children sitting around you on the mat and use a ball so that children can pass it onto their classmates one after another

The topic of the day ( Choose the topics from English, maths, EVS, GK, Language children studying for the month or choose any topic of your choice which is age appropriate)

Introductory Phase

Go through the five Circle Time skills with the children. The five skills of circle time: 1. Looking 2. Listening 3. Speaking 4. Thinking 5. Concentrating

Remember to praise kids to reinforce desired behavior: “Well done ………… for using good-looking skills.” A sample method to start the day:
Introduce the puppet:
  “Hello children I’d like you to meet ……… (Puppets name).  S/he would like to know what your names are”.
Ask each child in turn around the circle to say:
“Hello ………… (puppet’s name), my name is …………”

Middle Phase

Ask the children to sit with legs apart.  The children roll the ball to one another after saying: “Hello ….. (Name of the receiver)”.
The child who receives the ball responds by saying: “Thank you …….”

Closing Phase
Ask children to summarize the key points that were spoken in the Circle Time. If some kids are not able to start the conversation, help them by addressing them by name and ask questions to get answers from the child. Ensure all children clap for their classmate who spoke and answered. This helps children to be more open and take initiatives to speak during Circle Time and get rid of the stage fear.

Note for teachers
If some children are inattentive, ensure to make them part of the Circle Time and ask them questions to get desired answers. This will make the Circle Time more interactive and ensure full participation of the class. If a new rhyme has been taught, ask kids to sing the rhyme and some of the kids can make actions to gather the interest of all their classmates. Never forget to appreciate the kids who are taking initiatives to involve in the CT and for the kids who answered your questions. Encourage kids who are quiet to speak up in the class and tell them they can earn stars for mixing with their classmates!!!

Towards the end, clap for the children and ensure all clap for themselves… Eg: “You all have made great efforts to stay focused and it’s wonderful to see that all of you have been able to sit patiently and didn’t create any fuss in the class”.
